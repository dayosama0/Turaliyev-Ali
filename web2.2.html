Tribute to Mitski

About Mitski

Mitski Miyawaki (born 1990), known mononymously as Mitski, is a Japanese-American singer-songwriter and musician. She is known for her introspective lyrics, powerful vocal range, and genre-blending music that combines indie rock, folk, and electronic elements. Her deeply personal and emotionally resonant songs have made her one of the most critically acclaimed artists of her generation.

Early Life

Born in Japan to a Japanese mother and American father, Mitski spent her childhood moving frequently due to her father's job, living in various countries before settling in the United States for her education.

Achievements

  1. Released six critically acclaimed studio albums including "Bury Me at Makeout Creek" and "Be the Cowboy"
  2. Named one of the best new artists by numerous publications including Pitchfork and Rolling Stone
  3. Her album "Be the Cowboy" was listed as Album of the Year by multiple critics
  4. Performed at major music festivals including Coachella, Lollapalooza, and Pitchfork Music Festival
  5. Recognized for breaking barriers as an Asian-American woman in indie rock
  6. Built a devoted global fanbase through her emotionally honest songwriting and captivating live performances

Key Albums and Songs

  • "Lush" (2012) - Debut studio album
  • "Bury Me at Makeout Creek" (2014) - Breakthrough album
  • "Puberty 2" (2016) - Critical acclaim with hits like "Your Best American Girl"
  • "Be the Cowboy" (2018) - Commercial and critical success
  • "Laurel Hell" (2022) - Chart-topping album
  • "The Land Is Inhospitable and So Are We" (2023) - Latest release
